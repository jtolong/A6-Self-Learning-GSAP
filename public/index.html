Chapter 1 — From “meh” to motion

My pages worked—but they felt like PowerPoint slides. I wanted animation that guides attention, sets pace, and feels intentional.

Before

Tiny CSS fades, no sequencing, inconsistent timing.

After (goal)

Choreographed beats, scroll‑aware transitions, reusable timings.

Chapter 2 — Learning the verbs

GSAP clicked when I thought in verbs: move, fade, scale. Timelines let me stack those verbs into a sentence the browser can “perform.”

  • .from() for entrances that feel motivated
  • .to() for state changes without surprise
  • .timeline() to layer beats and overlap

Chapter 3 — Scroll as a remote

Pin: Keep the visual steady while text changes around it.

Start/End: Tune pacing like editing a video—frame by frame.

Scrub: Map progress to the user’s scroll for tactile control.

Chapter 4 — Make order visible

Staggering shows priority. Sequencing shows relationships. Together, they make content feel intentional.

Chapter 5 — Layout that morphs

Flip turns “teleporting” layouts into believable motion. The brain sees continuity, not chaos.

Chapter 6 — Ship for everyone

I tested the animations at phone and laptop sizes, and I respect prefers-reduced-motion. When motion is off, content still reads cleanly.
